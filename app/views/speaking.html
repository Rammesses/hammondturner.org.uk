You're the Tech Lead... you fix it!

  • Dotnet South West Meetup - March 2019

Over the last couple of years, I've presented 20 tricks and tips that I've found invaluable as a Tech Lead. But in this session, I want to turn things around and look at applying some of those to solving specific issues that are common within many software development teams.

I'll be taking lessons learned from the last 2 years of a major development project and using them to illustrate my points.

We'll look at managing your BAs and PMs and how to balance their need to understand exactly how long everything takes versus your team's need to not be over-pressured by artificial deadlines. We'll see how a structured approach to analysing problems encountered in LIVE can make everyone's life easier, and how to prove whether the problem is (or is not) the fault of your software. And how to handle that inevitable case where it is.

And we'll look at the problems of implementing uniformity across multiple code-bases and multiple teams delivering multiple releases of multiple features... and where getting your tooling right can give the biggest benefits.

Azure In Action - CosmosDB, Functions and ServiceBus in Perfect Harmony

Slide Deck
  • DDD South West 8 - April 2018

There's so many parts to Azure, it's sometimes hard to decide what to use and when. Do I use TableStore or CosmosDB? Would BlobStore be better? Should I host a full-fat .Net service in a VM, or stand up an ASP.Net Core WebApi? What about functions? The choices are myriad.

In this talk I describe how at Landmark we made these kind of decisions as we implemented some new features in our product. I talk about the questions you need to ask to make those decisions, where we went wrong, and how we succeeded in the end.

I describe in detail how we used CosmosDB, Azure Functions and Service Bus together to provide a Compliance Audit trail feature that would scale properly, work reliably, be trivial to use, and that wouldn't break the bank.

Honey, I shrunk `ConfigureServices()`

Blog Post
  • DDD Reading 2018 - June 2018 (10 minute Grok Talk)

The problem with all DI systems is that configuration generally is pulled in two polar-opposite directions. Either your container is configured by convention (with a few rare exception cases), or it's configured directly and declaratively with masses of registrations.

For the last few years, I've been using a discovery system for Unity that moves the registrations for dependencies away from the "God Configuration Method" and into individual "Bootstrappers" that live alongside the implementation of the service that they register.

In this Grok Talk, I introduce a MicrosoftDI version of the "Magic" discovery pattern and show how easy it is to shrink ConfigureServices.

Service Discovery with Consul.io for the .Net developer

Slide Deck
  • DDD South West 7 - May 2017

Microservice architectures are all the rage - hundreds of tiny services, all communicating - built, versioned and deployed independently. But even if your application only has a very few components, as soon as you go for High Availability you're already in that world.

Enter Consul.io - your saviour of service discovery.

In this session, I'll explain the High Availability architecture employed at Landmark, and how we've use Consul.io to provide discovery, monitoring and load-balancing for our services across an environment that spans two geographically separate data centres.

And as a bonus, you can potentially get rid of a LOAD of configuration in your app.config or web.config file.

Getting your Splunk on - a developer's guide to Splunk

Slide Deck
  • DDD 11 - Sept 2016

Splunk is a magical tool - it sucks in your logs, indexes them and gives you a rich search and data mining application to drill deep into them.

But as a developer why the heck would you want to use such a DevOps'y tool?

In this session, I show how using Splunk whilst developing can give insight into your code - how to time transactions, graph volumes and analyse performance - and how Splunking as a developer will make life in production that much easier.

An Introduction to Octopus Deployment

Slide Deck
  • DDD East Anglia - Sept 2013
  • DDD North - Oct 2013
  • Chester Devs - Mar 2014
  • Developer South Coast - Sept 2014
  • The Dev Bakery - April 2015

In an enterprise environment, publishing to live from the desktop really doesn't cut it - testing, customer approval and change tracking all require more checks, steps and environments than the usual Visual Studio or Azure tools provide.

Octopus Deploy is a NuGet-based system that allows great flexibility in managing deployments to numerous environments across multiple releases.

In this talk I give an introduction to Octopus Deploy, showing how it can be used to deploy a web site to a local IIS instance, a "test" server and up to Azure, and how it can be easily integrated with a CI build system to control releases, with "tales from the trenches".

Using your NuGet for fun and profit

Slide Deck
  • DDD South West 4 - May 2012
  • DDD North - Oct 2012

The explosive take-up of NuGet clearly demonstrates just how valuable a packaging system for .Net can be.

This session looks the issues surrounding creating NuGet packages for use within your own organisation rather than for general consumption, and will look at the options for hosting a NuGet server both on-site and in the cloud.

It also demonstrates the NuGet.PackageNPublish tooling released by Landmark at DDDSW4 which makes creating packages to contain your libraries as easy as File->New Project.
